# Human Capital Systems
## One-line positioning
Human Capital Systems explores how human potential can become visible, trusted
 and actionable.
## Core question
How can future value, contribution, skill, trust and potential be represented without reducing people to scores?
## Thesis
The next civilization will need better ways to recognize human potential before it is fully realized.
Today, funding, opportunity, reputation and trust often follow past credentials, institutional signals or existing networks. Human Capital Systems asks how AI, knowledge graphs, contribution records, reputation signals and social impact
 data could help identify and support future potential more intelligently.
## Why it matters
Many people have ideas, capabilities, care, imagination or latent contribution that existing systems do not see.
If AI can help map skills, ideas, trust, values, achievements, networks and future contribution, then new forms of matching, funding, collaboration and opportunity become possible.
The challenge is to do this without turning human beings into reductive scores, rankings or control systems.
## Possible components
- human potential graph
- idea graph
- contribution records
- reputation and trust signals
- future value creation index
- proof of human capital
- social impact memory
- actor-of-change profiles
- matching between ideas, builders, funders and communities
## Relationship to YOUniverse
YOUniverse is the broader ecosystem for human potential.
Human Capital Systems is one of its civilization-facing layers: the part that asks how people’s ideas, contributions, trust and future potential can become visible and exchangeable while remaining under human control.
## Design principle
The goal is not to rank humans.
The goal is to help potential become visible, supported and connected before existing institutions know how to recognize it.
## Open questions
- How can future potential be represented without becoming deterministic?
- What kinds of proof are meaningful but not oppressive?
- How can people control their own data and contribution graph?
- How
 can funders, builders and idea people be matched more intelligently?
- How can reputation systems avoid becoming social credit systems?
