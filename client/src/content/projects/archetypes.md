# Archetypes
## One-line positioning
Archetypes is a symbolic framework for universal structures of dreams, myths, stories and consciousness.
## Core question
What universal symbolic structures shape dreams, myths, religions, stories and inner life?
## Thesis
All dreams are variations of universal archetypal structures.
The Archetypes project explores the deeper grammar of symbolic experience: the recurring patterns, forces, figures and relationships that appear across dreams, myths, religions, cinema, psychology, spiritual visions and civilizational imaginaries.
It treats archetypes not as fixed labels, but as dynamic forces: cognitive attractors, symbolic operating systems and relational structures of consciousness.
## Why it matters
Modern culture often treats symbols as subjective decoration or psychological content. The Archetypes project asks whether symbols are also a shared architecture of human meaning.
If so, understanding archetypes can help humans navigate dreams, stories, spiritual experiences, cultural narratives and personal transformation with more depth and precision.
