# Stewart Brand

## Why He Matters
Brand's concept of pace layers — the idea that civilization changes at different speeds across fashion, commerce, infrastructure, governance, culture, and nature — is one of the most useful frameworks for thinking about civilizational change. His Whole Earth Catalog democratized access to tools and ideas decades before the internet.

## Connection to This Inquiry
Brand's long-term thinking and his insistence on the importance of infrastructure (not just innovation) directly inform the civilization layer of this inquiry. His work on how buildings learn — how structures adapt over time — is a metaphor for how civilizational systems should be designed.

## Key Ideas
- **Pace Layers**: Different layers of civilization change at different speeds; fast layers innovate, slow layers stabilize.
- **Long Now**: The importance of thinking on 10,000-year timescales.
- **Whole Earth**: The democratization of tools, ideas, and access.
- **How Buildings Learn**: Structures that adapt over time are more alive than those that don't.
