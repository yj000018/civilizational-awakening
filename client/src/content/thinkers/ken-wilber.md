# Ken Wilber

## Why He Matters
Wilber created the most comprehensive map of human consciousness and development available in contemporary philosophy. His Integral Theory synthesizes Eastern and Western wisdom traditions, developmental psychology, systems theory, and contemplative practice into a single navigable framework.

## Connection to This Inquiry
The Civilizational Awakening inquiry draws on Wilber's insistence that any adequate map of human potential must include interior dimensions — not just exterior behavior or social structures. The five pillars of this inquiry (Spirit/Soul, Heart, Mind, Body/Action, Civilization) reflect an integral structure.

## Key Ideas
- **AQAL**: All Quadrants, All Levels — the four fundamental dimensions of any phenomenon: interior/exterior × individual/collective.
- **Spectrum of Consciousness**: Human development moves through identifiable stages, each transcending and including the previous.
- **Lines of Development**: Intelligence is not one thing — cognitive, emotional, moral, spiritual lines develop semi-independently.
- **States and Stages**: Temporary peak experiences (states) are distinct from permanent developmental attainments (stages).
