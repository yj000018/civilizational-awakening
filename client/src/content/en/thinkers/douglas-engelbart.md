# Douglas Engelbart

## Why He Matters
Engelbart's 1968 "Mother of All Demos" introduced the mouse, hypertext, video conferencing, and collaborative editing — decades before they became mainstream. His deeper vision was not about technology but about augmenting human intellect: using computers to help humans think, coordinate, and solve complex problems together.

## Connection to This Inquiry
Engelbart is the direct ancestor of Y-OS and Memory OS. His question — how do we augment human intellect? — is the founding question of the Mind pillar. His concept of "bootstrapping" (using the system to improve the system) is a core principle of Y-OS.

## Key Ideas
- **Augmenting Human Intellect**: Technology should amplify human cognitive capacity, not replace it.
- **Bootstrapping**: Using improved tools to improve the tools themselves.
- **Collective IQ**: The intelligence of a group can be systematically enhanced.
- **NLS/Augment**: His pioneering collaborative knowledge system, decades ahead of its time.
