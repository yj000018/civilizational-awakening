# Christopher Alexander

## Why He Matters
Alexander spent his career asking why some buildings and cities feel alive while others feel dead. His answer — that living structure emerges from specific geometric and relational patterns — led to the development of pattern languages, which later influenced software architecture and the design of complex systems.

## Connection to This Inquiry
Alexander's insistence that design must serve human wholeness — not efficiency, not aesthetics, not ideology — is a direct ancestor of the civilization layer of this inquiry. His pattern language methodology is a model for how to design civilizational infrastructure that supports human flourishing.

## Key Ideas
- **Pattern Language**: A structured vocabulary of design patterns that can be combined to create living environments.
- **Centers**: Living structure is made of nested, overlapping centers that reinforce each other.
- **Living Structure**: Some structures support life and wholeness; others suppress it.
- **The Nature of Order**: A four-volume exploration of the deep structure of beauty and life in the built world.
