# Iain McGilchrist

## Why He Matters
McGilchrist's central argument is that the left hemisphere of the brain — which is analytic, reductive, and control-oriented — has come to dominate Western civilization, suppressing the right hemisphere's capacity for wholeness, context, and presence. The result is a civilization that excels at manipulation but struggles with meaning.

## Connection to This Inquiry
McGilchrist's diagnosis of left-hemisphere dominance is one of the most important frames for understanding why civilization needs awakening. His work directly informs the Spirit/Soul and Mind pillars, and the question of what kind of intelligence AI should amplify.

## Key Ideas
- **Divided Brain**: The two hemispheres of the brain have fundamentally different ways of attending to the world.
- **Left Hemisphere Dominance**: Modern civilization has systematically privileged analytic, reductive, control-oriented thinking.
- **Attention Shapes Reality**: How we attend to the world determines what world we inhabit.
- **The Emissary and the Master**: The left hemisphere (emissary) has usurped the right hemisphere (master).
