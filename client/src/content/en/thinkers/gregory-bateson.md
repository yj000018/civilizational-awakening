# Gregory Bateson

## Why He Matters
Bateson was one of the most original thinkers of the 20th century. He worked across anthropology, cybernetics, psychiatry, and ecology to develop a unified theory of mind and nature. His insistence that mind is not located in the brain but distributed across systems is foundational for understanding collective intelligence and AI.

## Connection to This Inquiry
Bateson's "ecology of mind" — the idea that mental processes are embedded in larger ecological and social systems — is a key frame for the civilization and network layers of this inquiry. His concept of "the pattern which connects" is a poetic description of what this entire inquiry is trying to map.

## Key Ideas
- **The Pattern Which Connects**: There is a meta-pattern connecting all living things.
- **Ecology of Mind**: Mind is not in the head but distributed across systems and relationships.
- **Double Bind**: A communication pattern that creates irresolvable contradictions — relevant to organizational and civilizational dysfunction.
- **Levels of Learning**: Learning itself can be learned; meta-learning is the deepest form of adaptation.
