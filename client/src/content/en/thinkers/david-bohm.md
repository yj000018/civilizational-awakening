# David Bohm

## Why He Matters
Bohm was one of the most original physicists of the 20th century. He proposed that beneath the explicate order of observable reality lies an implicate order — a deeper, undivided wholeness. His work on dialogue as a collective thinking process has influenced organizational learning, conflict resolution, and collective intelligence.

## Connection to This Inquiry
Bohm's insistence on wholeness — that fragmentation of thought is the root of human problems — resonates deeply with the Civilizational Awakening inquiry. His dialogue practice is a direct ancestor of the collective intelligence and network/transmission layers.

## Key Ideas
- **Implicate Order**: The deeper, enfolded order from which the explicate (observable) world unfolds.
- **Wholeness**: Reality is fundamentally undivided; fragmentation is a feature of thought, not of nature.
- **Dialogue**: A form of collective thinking in which participants suspend assumptions and explore meaning together.
- **Rheomode**: A proposed grammar in which verbs, not nouns, are primary — reflecting the flowing nature of reality.
