# Albert Einstein

## Why He Matters
Einstein's scientific work transformed humanity's understanding of space, time, matter, and energy. But his humanist philosophy — his insistence that scientists bear moral responsibility for the consequences of their discoveries, his advocacy for world government, his belief that imagination is more important than knowledge — is equally relevant to this inquiry.

## Connection to This Inquiry
Einstein's statement that "we cannot solve our problems with the same thinking we used when we created them" is one of the founding principles of the Civilizational Awakening inquiry. His combination of rigorous scientific thinking with deep humanist concern is a model for how to approach the age of AI.

## Key Ideas
- **Relativity**: Space and time are not absolute but relative to the observer.
- **Imagination Over Knowledge**: "Imagination is more important than knowledge. Knowledge is limited. Imagination encircles the world."
- **Moral Responsibility**: Scientists and technologists bear responsibility for the uses of their discoveries.
- **World Government**: Einstein advocated for supranational governance to prevent war and manage technology.
