# Collective Intelligence
## One-line positioning
Collective Intelligence explores how intelligence coordinates at collective scale.
## Core question
How can human and artificial intelligence coordinate toward wiser collective action?
## Thesis
The future will not be shaped only by individual intelligence or individual AI assistants. It will be shaped by how people, groups, institutions, networks and AI systems think together.
Collective Intelligence asks how knowledge, trust, memory, deliberation, prediction, contribution and action can be coordinated across many minds and
 many tools.
## Why it matters
Many of humanity’s hardest problems are coordination problems.
We have knowledge, talent, goodwill and technical capacity, but often lack systems that help groups sense reality, deliberate well, remember decisions, allocate resources, trust each other and act coherently.
AI can either amplify fragmentation or help build better collective sensemaking and action.
## Possible components
- collective sensemaking
- group memory
- deliberation tools
- prediction
 and belief systems
- reputation and trust graphs
- idea markets
- expert networks
- civic coordination tools
- AI-assisted research groups
- decision and action protocols
## Relationship to Next Civ
Next Civ asks what civilization-scale systems are needed for the future.
Collective Intelligence is one of its core capabilities: the ability for groups and societies to perceive, think, decide and act together with greater wisdom.
## Design principle
Collective intelligence is not only aggregation.
It is the design of conditions where diverse perspectives, knowledge, care and intelligence can become coherent without becoming centralized control.
## Open questions
- How can AI improve collective sensemaking without manipulating consensus?
- What kinds of memory do groups need?
- How can trust be represented without becoming surveillance?
- How can prediction, deliberation and action be connected?
- What would a truly wise network look like?
